## 2-Day Response Validation (Before Planning Next Activity)

This 2-day plan is a validation sprint, not a full rollout.

**Primary objective:**
- Measure real response from companies in Tower B3
- Capture what works and what does not by company type
- Use field data to plan the next phase

**What to measure by end of Monday:**
- Signups, warm leads, and follow-up commitments
- Top objections and top feature requests
- Which target segments respond fastest (B2B, B2C, product, freelancer, enterprise)

**How to plan the next activity (Tuesday onward):**
- **Strong response:** Expand to remaining floors/towers with the same core flow
- **Mixed response:** Keep the flow, refine opening lines and segment-specific messaging
- **Weak response:** Tighten value proposition and run one more short validation cycle before scaling

**Rule:** No scale decision without reviewing Monday response data first.

## Target Audience Segments & Pitch Scenarios

Spaze App is for all company types in the building:
- B2B service providers (IT, legal, HR, consulting, printing)
- B2C businesses (retail, F&B, wellness, clinics)
- Product companies (startups, manufacturers, distributors)
- Freelancers and small teams
- Large enterprises and anchor tenants

**How to adapt your pitch:**
- Ask what the company does and who their typical clients are.
- Use these pitch examples:
  - **B2B Service Provider:** “Many companies here need services like yours but don’t know you’re in the building. Spaze makes you visible to every potential client next door.”
  - **B2C Business:** “Employees in this building are always looking for food, wellness, or shopping options nearby. Spaze helps them discover your business instantly.”
  - **Product Company:** “Other companies may need your products or can refer you. Spaze lets you list everything you offer so you’re top of mind for local demand.”
  - **Freelancer/Small Team:** “It’s hard to get noticed in a big building. Spaze puts you on the map so companies can find your skills easily.”
  - **Large Enterprise:** “Even big companies need local vendors and services. Spaze helps your team find trusted providers right in the building.”

**Tip:** Always relate the pitch to their business type and what they might need or offer to others in the building.

**Step 3: Set up tracking sheet**

  - *"What would make this app most useful for you?"* (open text)
  - *"Any concerns about joining?"* (open text)

**Step 5: Load mockup/screenshots on your phone**

- Prepare 3–4 screens that show: (1) company listing view, (2) search by category, (3) company profile with services, (4) browsing results
- If someone says *"Show me it working"* — be honest: *"We're building it right now. This is the design. We're talking to companies first to make sure we build the right thing — that's why I'm here."*

## Rules for Both Days

1. **Never spend more than 5 minutes per office** unless they invite you to sit
2. **Always log the visit immediately** — don't trust your memory
3. **Always ask for THEIR visiting card** — this is your follow-up channel if they don't fill the form
4. **Offer to fill the form FOR them** — *"I'll add you right now, just tell me what you do"* removes all friction
5. **Show, don't tell** — mockup on phone > words. If they say "show me it working" — be honest it's pre-build and that's WHY you're here
6. **Name-drop carefully** — only mention companies that agreed to be referenced
7. **Don't argue with "no"** — say "no problem", hand your card (Spaze info on back), move on
8. **Receptionists are allies** — be warm, remember their names, they'll mention you to the boss
9. **End every conversation with a clear next step** — signup, follow-up time, or referral
10. **Let feedback come naturally** — don't interrogate. If they mention a pain point or need, note it after you leave. Best feedback is stuff they say without being asked.
11. **Nitin Sir is representing the neighbouring tower to build a community** — not to sell aggressively
12. **3 signups on Day 1 is a win** — don't measure success by volume, measure by quality of conversations and cards collected
