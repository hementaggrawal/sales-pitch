# Spaze App: Door-to-Door Pitch Guide

## What is Spaze App?
Spaze App is a free, community-driven platform designed for office buildings with hundreds of companies. It helps everyone onboard, share their offerings, post requests, and connect with others who can help or collaborate. Our mission is to break barriers and foster a connected, thriving business ecosystem within your building.

**Q: Can I opt out or delete my data?**
A: Yes, you can opt out or request data deletion at any time. We respect your privacy and choices.

**Q: What if I have a suggestion or want a new feature?**
A: We welcome feedback! You can suggest features or improvements directly in the app or by contacting our team.

## Pitching to Different People & Scenarios

### 1. At the Reception/Front Desk
- Be polite and brief. Introduce yourself and the purpose: “Hi, I’m here to share information about a free app that helps companies in this building connect and grow.”
- Ask if you can leave a flyer or request a meeting with the office manager or decision-maker.
- Respect their process—if they ask for an appointment, thank them and follow up as directed.

### 2. Office Manager/Admin
- Focus on benefits for the whole company: “Spaze App helps your team discover new opportunities, find services, and connect with others in the building—all for free.”
- Offer a quick demo or leave materials for review.
- Address concerns about privacy, security, and management approval.

### 3. Business Owners/Decision Makers
- Highlight business growth, networking, and visibility: “This is a chance to showcase your company’s strengths and find new clients or partners right here.”
- Emphasize that it’s free and community-driven.
- Be ready to answer questions about your motivation and the app’s benefits.

### 4. Employees/Team Members
- Focus on personal convenience: “You can find help, share needs, or discover offers from other companies in the building.”
- Encourage them to share the app with their colleagues or management.

### General Tips
- Always be respectful of time and company policies.
- Have printed materials or a QR code ready.
- Be prepared for questions or to schedule a follow-up.
- If not allowed to pitch, thank them and move on politely.
