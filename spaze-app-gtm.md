# Spaze App Go-To-Market (GTM) Plan

## Execution Owner
- Nitin Sir will execute this GTM on the ground.
- He should carry a Jina Code Systems visiting card from Tapan Sir or Amit Sir with Spaze details added on the back.
- His role is to start conversations, build trust, collect company details, and bring companies into the founding list.

## 1. Target Audience
Spaze App is designed for all types of companies and professionals in large office buildings (500+ companies), including:
- B2B service providers (e.g., IT, legal, HR, consulting, printing)
- B2C businesses (e.g., retail, F&B, wellness, clinics)
- Product companies (startups, manufacturers, distributors)
- Freelancers and small teams
- Large enterprises and anchor tenants
- Office managers, business owners, and team members

**How to identify and adapt:**
- Ask what the company does and who their typical clients are.
- Use the scenario-based pitch below to tailor your message.

## 2. Value Proposition
- Free, easy onboarding for all
- Connect, share, and discover opportunities within the building
- Breaks silos, increases visibility, and builds a supportive business community
- This is not a hard-sell app pitch; it is a building-connection initiative that helps companies discover and approach each other more easily

## 3. GTM Flow
1. Start with the building problem: companies in the same tower do not know who does what.
2. Explain Spaze as a free building directory that helps companies connect with nearby businesses.
3. Show how it helps both sides: find vendors faster and become discoverable inside the building.
4. Ask for company details or waitlist signup on the spot.
5. Collect feedback, note objections, and identify high-interest companies for follow-up.
6. Revisit warm leads and use collected cards to continue the relationship.

## 3a. Scenario-Based Pitch Examples

- **B2B Service Provider:**
	- "Many companies here need services like yours but don’t know you’re in the building. Spaze makes you visible to every potential client next door."
- **B2C Business:**
	- "Employees in this building are always looking for food, wellness, or shopping options nearby. Spaze helps them discover your business instantly."
- **Product Company:**
	- "Other companies may need your products or can refer you. Spaze lets you list everything you offer so you’re top of mind for local demand."
- **Freelancer/Small Team:**
	- "It’s hard to get noticed in a big building. Spaze puts you on the map so companies can find your skills easily."
- **Large Enterprise:**
	- "Even big companies need local vendors and services. Spaze helps your team find trusted providers right in the building."

**Tip:** Always relate the pitch to their business type and what they might need or offer to others in the building.

## 4. Key Channels
- Door-to-door pitching (using the pitch guide)
- Visiting cards with Spaze context and QR code (primary leave-behind for field visits)
- QR codes for instant app access
- Email outreach to office managers and HR
- Building management collaboration (if possible)
- Word-of-mouth and employee referrals

## 4a. Pilot-to-Scale Rule
- Run the 2-day GTM sprint first to validate real response before full expansion.
- Decide next activity only after reviewing response data: signups, objections, referrals, and segment-wise traction.

## 5. Launch Activities
- Soft launch with a few early adopter companies
- Collect testimonials and feedback
- Organize a launch event or demo day in the building
- Offer onboarding support (in-person or virtual)
- Run a contest or incentive for first sign-ups

## 6. Engagement & Retention
- Regular in-app updates and feature announcements
- Community spotlights (feature companies or users)
- Feedback channels for suggestions and improvements
- Ongoing support and troubleshooting

## 7. Metrics to Track
- Number of companies onboarded
- Active users per week/month
- Number of posts, requests, and connections made
- User feedback and satisfaction
- Referral and invite rates

## 8. Potential Hurdles & Solutions
- **Low adoption:** Use testimonials, incentives, and personal outreach
- **Management resistance:** Emphasize community benefits, offer demos, and address concerns
- **Privacy concerns:** Highlight security features and data protection
- **Sustaining engagement:** Regular updates, community events, and recognition

## 9. Long-Term Vision
- Expand to other buildings and business parks
- Add advanced features based on user needs
- Build a trusted, city-wide business community

## Why Spaze App Will Succeed

- **Solves a Real Problem:** Office buildings with hundreds of companies often lack a simple way to connect, collaborate, and share opportunities. Spaze App fills this gap.
- **Community-Driven & Free:** No cost or barriers to entry encourages rapid adoption and trust.
- **Relationship-First Approach:** The GTM is based on real conversations, trust, and building-to-building connections, not on aggressive selling.
- **Network Effect:** The more companies join, the more valuable the platform becomes for everyone.
- **Easy Onboarding:** Simple registration and intuitive features make it accessible to all employees, not just tech-savvy users.
- **Strong Value Proposition:** Saves time, increases visibility, and helps companies grow their business and network.
- **Scalable Model:** Can be replicated in other buildings and business parks, expanding impact quickly.
- **Continuous Feedback Loop:** Early user feedback and ongoing improvements ensure the app stays relevant and useful.
